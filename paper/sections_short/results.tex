We report three pre-registered findings. F1 establishes a scale-dependent effect of the pretraining objective and is the principal positive result; F2 tests kāraka role-stratified masking at matched budget; F3 tests a supervised kāraka-role auxiliary objective across five seeds. Together they localize the robust gains to architecture (RoPE) and objective design (pure MLM at scale), while the Pāṇinian mechanisms add interpretability without a validated BLiMP improvement at this scale. Figure~\ref{fig:forest} places all four pre-registered effects on a common axis. We first situate the two submission models on the official BabyLM~2026 leaderboard harness (Table~\ref{tab:official}); all controlled comparisons (F1--F3) then use a single internal harness that scores the same checkpoints about five to six points higher, a systematic offset we report transparently (Table~\ref{tab:headline}).

\subsection{F1: Scale-Dependent Objective Effect}

A hybrid objective adds a causal language-modelling (CLM) head to the MLM loss, which may stabilize learning when the MLM signal is sparse but dilute it once MLM is strong. Pure-MLM and hybrid models are trained identically except for the objective, with three seeds at the 10M-token budget and single seeds at 100M. At 10M, pure-MLM achieves a 3-seed mean of 63.58 (95\% CI $\pm$1.73) versus hybrid 64.09 ($\pm$0.26), with overlapping intervals. At 100M, pure-MLM reaches 73.06 against hybrid 67.57, a 5.49-point advantage (Figure~\ref{fig:objscale}). The CLM head's regularizing effect at 10M becomes a bottleneck on MLM signal quality at the larger budget. This speaks directly to GPT-BERT~\cite{CharpentierSamuel2024}, whose hybrid masked-plus-causal training won BabyLM~2024: the hybrid recipe is not uniformly preferable, and at the 100M-token budget pure-MLM is the stronger choice on BLiMP. The 10M neutrality justifies retaining hybrid for the Strict-Small submission; the 100M result drives the Strict-track model.

\label{fig:objscale}\end{figure}

\subsection{Strict-Track Results: prabhasa-b\_s (100M, Pure-MLM)}

The 100M-token pure-MLM model (prabhasa-b\_s, n\,=\,1) is evaluated on the full Strict-track suite (baseline 74.53 BLiMP). It trails on main BLiMP by 1.47 points (73.06 versus 74.53) but gains 2.46 points on BLiMP Supplement (67.46 versus 65.0) and 9.66 points on Entity Tracking (33.26 versus 23.6). Its Text Average of 55.99 exceeds the reference of 54.0, indicating competitive overall generalization despite the main-BLiMP shortfall. Per-category detail appears in Appendix~\ref{app:repro}. We report this single-seed figure transparently: re-running the locked recipe from identical initialization under two further seeds settles at 53.6 and 58.3 BLiMP (best loss 1.6--1.8 versus the submission seed's 0.515), with one optimizer trajectory diverging, so the 100M recipe is not yet robust to the training seed and 73.06 is a valid but fortunate-seed outcome (Appendix~\ref{app:repro}). The evaluation is not implicated: the submission checkpoint re-evaluates to 73.06 and an independent proxy reproduces all three scores within 0.4 points.

\subsection{F2: Kāraka-Masking Causality (Matched-Budget)}

Kāraka role-stratified masking (Arm K) is compared to uniform masking (Arm C) at identical mask budget (100M tokens, n\,=\,1 per arm), holding N-hot embeddings, RoPE, and pure-MLM fixed and disabling frequency-weighted masking ($\alpha = 0$) in both arms. Per pre-registration (cycle 21), the primary metric is a paired bootstrap over the 20 targeted paradigms. Arm K achieves 71.77 full BLiMP and 82.03 targeted; Arm C achieves 70.49 and 81.93. The targeted contrast is $\Delta_{K-C} = +0.10$ points (95\% CI $[-0.99, +1.20]$), not significant (Figure~\ref{fig:f2_karaka}). Earlier exploratory runs reported a $+1.23$-point gain, but that reflected unmatched mask rates and frequency weighting; with matching enforced, the role distribution contributes no signal. F2 is a pre-registered null; further interventions (real dependency-parsed roles, 3-seed replication) are queued.

\label{fig:f2_karaka}\end{figure}

\subsection{F3: Kāraka Auxiliary Objective (5-Seed Paired t-Test)}

The supervised kāraka-role head is applied at the 10M-token budget ($\lambda = 1.0$ versus baseline $\lambda = 0.0$), with corpus, hybrid objective, uniform masking, and seed list held fixed. Per pre-registration (cycle 6, extended cycle 60), the primary metric is the per-seed targeted delta. The 5-seed targeted mean is auxiliary 74.02 versus baseline 73.26 ($+0.76$ points, 95\% CI $[-0.74, +2.27]$; $t = 1.41$, df\,=\,4, not significant). The effect attenuates with sample size, from $+2.65$ at one seed to $+1.46$ at three to $+0.76$ at five (Figure~\ref{fig:f3}), so the seed-0 result was seed luck. A specificity control sharpens the reading: a shuffled-role auxiliary (3-seed targeted mean 73.53) does \emph{not} reproduce the real-auxiliary effect (74.41), which rules out generic multi-task regularization and is consistent with a weak kāraka-specific signal that is underpowered at 10M. On full 67-paradigm BLiMP the auxiliary reaches 64.89 versus baseline 63.88 ($+1.01$, ns).

\label{fig:f3}\end{figure}

\subsection{Strict-Small Track and GLUE}

The 10M-token submission (prabhasa-b\_ss-0.1, n\,=\,3) achieves a 3-seed mean of 64.09 BLiMP (95\% CI $\pm$0.26) against the Strict-Small baseline of 65.08, within a point. The corresponding pure-MLM ablation arm (mean 63.58; seeds 65.22/63.31/62.20) is reported separately under F1 and is not the submission. Fine-tuned on seven GLUE tasks~\cite{Wang2019GLUE}, the model reaches a macro-average of 0.581 (strongest on WSC 0.673 and MRPC 0.813 F1; weakest on MNLI 0.342), recovering on binary and structured-matching tasks but struggling with multi-class inference, as expected at the 10M-token budget (Figure~\ref{fig:glue}; per-task detail in Appendix~\ref{app:repro}).

\caption{GLUE per-task performance for prabhasa-b\_ss-0.1 (10M tokens, macro-average 0.581).}
\label{fig:glue}\end{figure}

Taken together, the three findings localize the robust gains to RoPE and the pure-MLM objective at scale, while the Pāṇinian masking and auxiliary mechanisms contribute role-based structure and design clarity without a validated BLiMP improvement at this scale.
