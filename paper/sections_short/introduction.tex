The scaling of language model pretraining has driven rapid progress in natural language understanding, yet the relationship between data efficiency, inductive bias, and model behaviour remains underspecified at smaller scales. Small language models under strict computational budgets offer a controlled testbed for understanding which architectural and training choices yield measurable improvements, unclouded by the confounds that emerge at frontier scale. This work investigates whether linguistic structure from classical Pāṇinian grammar, developed over more than a millennium~\cite{Matilal1990WordWorld}, operationalized through morpheme-boundary embeddings and role-aware masking, improves data-efficient pretraining of small models within the BabyLM constrained-data setting~\cite{Warstadt2023}.

Pāṇinian grammar encodes compositional structure at the morpheme level through inflectional rules and at the argument level through kāraka categories that denote semantic roles. The hypothesis is intuitive: if a linguistically grounded prior reduces the effective hypothesis space, the model should require fewer tokens to achieve given generalization. Prabhāsa investigates this through three research hypotheses. RQ-A (masking causality) tests whether kāraka role-stratified token masking, at matched mask-rate budget, carries causal efficacy on targeted BLiMP paradigms, operationalized via morpheme-boundary N-hot embeddings~\cite{Prasad2024Vidyut}. RQ-B (auxiliary objective) tests whether supervised auxiliary prediction of kāraka roles improves BLiMP performance when masking alone is ineffective. H2 (synergy, in scope) asks whether a Pañcāvayava fine-tuning scaffold improves reasoning quality on the best validated arm. H3 (epistemic constraint, out of scope) concerns constructive validity enforcement via Z3 verifiers. The primary evaluation focuses on H1 with pre-registered thresholds of at least plus 1.0 percentage point gain on targeted subsets, assessed at least 3 independent seeds using paired bootstrap resampling and Holm-Bonferroni correction.

This work contributes through mechanistic decomposition and statistical discipline. First, RQ-A and RQ-B are isolated using budget-matched arms to rule out confounds such as mask-rate variation that plagued prior comparisons of linguistically motivated masking~\cite{Yaltik2020, Botha2014Morphology}. Second, an incidental finding revealed that the choice between pure masked-language modelling (MLM) and hybrid MLM plus causal-language-modelling (CLM) is scale-dependent, with performance diverging significantly across model scales; Section~\ref{sec:results} reports the quantitative effect sizes and confidence intervals for this scale-dependent trade-off. Third, all hypothesis tests employ at least 3 independent random seeds, paired bootstrap resampling, and explicit pre-registration of thresholds. Fourth, code, data, and model checkpoints are released openly with full hyperparameter disclosure (see Appendix~\ref{app:repro}).

The empirical findings show that architecture (RoPE positional encoding~\cite{Su2021RoPE}) and objective design (pure MLM at scale) are the primary levers for improving BabyLM performance at 10M–100M parameters. The Pāṇinian mechanisms exhibit null or marginal effects under proper controls. Section~\ref{sec:results} reports the pre-registered effect sizes, confidence intervals, and significance tests for both RQ-A (masking causality) and RQ-B (auxiliary objective), demonstrating that neither mechanism achieves the pre-registered threshold for a positive finding when proper multi-seed validation and multiple-comparisons correction are applied. A well-documented null is a valid scientific contribution that constrains claims about structure-driven pretraining and identifies the true levers.
